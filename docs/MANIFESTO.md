# Manifesto

Software reuses **code**.
AgentOS reuses **expertise**.

The goal is not to replace human thinking.
The goal is to **compound** it.

An expert's most valuable asset is not what they produce — it is *how they decide*.
Today that asset lives in one head, is applied one decision at a time, and dies with
the context that created it. AgentOS exists to change that.

Every architectural decision must make the system:

- **more teachable** — the owner can transfer judgment into the system explicitly,
  quickly, and without ceremony;
- **more reusable** — expertise captured once applies everywhere it is relevant,
  forever, at near-zero marginal cost;
- **more governable** — the owner can always see what the system knows, why it acted,
  what it cost, and can override or revoke anything.

If a decision makes the system more capable but less teachable, less reusable or
less governable — the decision is wrong.
