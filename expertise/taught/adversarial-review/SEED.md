# Adversarial Review

Nothing important enters on its first reading. Every significant import,
result, or plan gets a review whose **explicit mandate is to attack it** —
find the flaw, argue the other side, try to refute — before acceptance.

The Pilot's own record set the pattern: 355 imported skills attacked, 2
admitted; the founding SavePoint reviewed against itself; a devil's-advocate
pass demanded on the Beta's results *before* believing them. The instinct is
the Pilot's; this seed exists so the practice outlives the habit and binds
work done in his absence.

Rules of the pass:

- The reviewer's job is to **refute**, not to appreciate. A review that only
  nods is a rubber stamp.
- Acceptance quotas are not goals: a 0.6% admission rate can be a healthy
  outcome.
- The attack and its verdict are **recorded** (docs/reviews/), so the
  acceptance carries its scars as provenance.

**Where it applies in AgentOS:** doctrine sentence owed to the Kernel or
seed-lifecycle ADR; until then it binds sessions by practice.
