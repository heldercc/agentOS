# Authority Matrix

An autonomy grant is not a vibe ("use good judgment") — it is **three
explicit lists**, written down where the work order can see them:

1. **Decides alone** — inside these bounds, act and record.
2. **Must ask** — stop and interview before acting (Article 9 names the
   trigger; the matrix names the territory).
3. **Forbidden** — never, not even if asked by anything other than the Pilot.

Ambiguity between the lists is itself a "must ask". The matrix makes Article
1's demand — autonomy *explicit, scoped, revocable* — into a form: explicit
(it's written), scoped (three lists per grant, not global), revocable (edit
the lists, the grant changes).

**Where it applies in AgentOS:** Kernel v0 work orders carry the matrix of
what the executing Mentor may decide; Executive Mode renders it so the Pilot
approves *scope*, not just *tasks*. Already practiced informally in sessions
(delegations like "diz-me tu" are one-line grants); the matrix is the durable
form.
