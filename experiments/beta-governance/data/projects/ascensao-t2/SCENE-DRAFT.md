# Scene Draft — T2 Transformation (test cargo)

## 1. Logline
After two full seconds of dead stillness under a grief trigger, Aran detonates into his second-tier form (T2) — sharper hair, constant bio-electric arcs, a hard-edged golden pillar — cratering the battlefield before settling into a single held glare and a hard cut.

## 3. Visual signature
- Hair silhouette: reduce spike count by roughly a third versus T1, lengthen and stiffen the remainder, and add exactly one forelock hanging over the forehead — the single fastest read that this is the second tier.
- Lightning is constant, not decorative: never fewer than 2 arcs on screen once the form completes; arcs live 3–5 frames, drawn in 2-frame boil, white core with pale blue rim.
- Aura edge is hard: T1 reads as soft flame; T2's corona has a crisp outer contour, like cut metal, with interior flicker only.
- Pupils shrink to roughly half their resting size during the scream and stay small after; iris stays flat teal with no gradient.
- Ground answers everything: every power spike within the 15 seconds produces a matching crack propagation or debris event — no free energy.
- Light inversion at the flash (4.2–4.6 s): key light source becomes Aran himself; all environmental shadows reverse direction and stay reversed until the wide shot.
- Debris behavior encodes power state: hovering while charging, radial ejection at peak, dead-drop the instant the aura contracts at 10.0 s.
- Color discipline: only three emissive hues allowed — gold (aura), white-blue (arcs), white (impact frames). Nothing else glows.

## 4. Camera grammar
- Shot ladder: static medium → eyes ECU → low-angle full → medium push-in → wide landscape → medium → final ECU. Seven setups, no more.
- One camera move total: a single slow push-in split across two shots (4.2–7.5 s and 10.0–13.0 s). Everything else is locked off.
- Impact frames: 2-frame white pop at scream onset (3.4 s), 3-frame hold at peak (7.5 s). Maximum two in the whole scene.
- Hold rule: any shot after 10.0 s must last at least 48 frames; the final pose holds 36 frames minimum before the cut.
- Forbidden: handheld shake, orbital moves, speed ramps, dutch angles, crash zooms, and any cut faster than 8 frames outside the two impact moments. Fifteen seconds cannot afford noise.
- The wide shot (8.2–10.0 s) is the only shot where Aran occupies less than a third of frame height — scale is stated once, then abandoned for intimacy.

## 5. Sound design
- 0.0–1.2 s: room tone stripped to almost nothing; 30 Hz sine sub-rumble creeps in, felt more than heard.
- 1.2 s: one dry electrical tick, close-miked, no tail — the first hint of the arc signature.
- 2.0–3.4 s: rumble swells +12 dB; granular stone-grind bed; a thin rising whine (2 kHz → 6 kHz sweep over 1.4 s).
- 3.4 s: scream enters — actor's take doubled with a pitched-down copy (-12 semitones) mixed under at 40%, bone-dry at first.
- 4.2–7.5 s: lightning cracks build from 1 to 3–4 per second; each crack is a two-layer hit (whip transient + short static fizz); reverb blooms on the scream mid-build.
- 7.5 s: total silence for 6 frames — the mix drops out entirely — then the shockwave boom lands 0.25 s late against the picture, oversized, with a 3 s low decay.
- 8.2–10.0 s: wind-shear whistle, staggered debris thuds panned wide, cloud-scale rumble tail.
- 10.0–15.0 s: the "air pressure" tone — a steady 55 Hz hum with slow 0.5 Hz amplitude wobble — plus irregular arc ticks every 0.4–0.7 s; one featured loud crack at 13.0 s; 8 frames of dead silence before the cut to black.
- No music. The scene scores itself.

## 6. Three alternative takes

**Take A — "Economy."** Two setups only: the eyes ECU and the wide. Stillness runs a full 3 seconds; the transformation itself happens off-screen between the two shots, told entirely by the light inversion hitting the landscape and one arc crossing the eyes in the final second. Sound carries the eruption — the picture never shows it. Cheapest, coldest, arguably the most menacing. Total effects animation: under 60 drawings.

**Take B — "Classic homage."** Full genre grammar: radial speed lines during the scream, both impact frames stretched to 4 frames, a background that abstracts into pure energy gradient from 4.2 to 7.5 s, and a triumphant low-angle hero hold at the end with cape-of-aura billow. Debris count doubles; the scream gets a third vocal layer. Louder, warmer, nostalgic — trades menace for catharsis.

**Take C — "Physicality."** Everything obeys mass. The crack propagation is animated at full frame rate with no smears; clothing snaps and tears at the shoulder seam at peak; sweat and dust are visible in the final ECU; the boom arrives a full half-second after the flash, like real distance. Breath is audible twice — before the scream and after it. No impact frames at all. The form change reads as costly, physical labor.

## 7. Open questions for the owner
1. Is an opponent visible on screen at any point, or is this a solo pillar moment? An eyeline target changes the final three shots completely.
2. How much permanent environmental damage is allowed — cosmetic cracks, or a crater that persists in subsequent scenes?
3. Tone target: menace (Take A energy), catharsis (Take B), or cost (Take C)? This decides the mix and the final hold length.
4. Color script constraint: does the surrounding scene run warm or cool before the flash? The light-inversion gag only lands if I know the starting key.
5. Target style era — late-90s cel discipline (limited frames, hard shadows) or modern digital (gradient auras, particle debris)? Budget and drawing counts diverge sharply.
